Recycling Project
"I had a dream..."

INSTALLATION, 2016

“Recycling saves energy, reduces landfills, preserves our resources, and protects nature.” Everyone may heard of this at least once in their life times. However, not many people do recycle due to their laziness and lack of knowledge. Through I had a dream…, I wanted to give new experience to audiences about recycling and tell that anyone can help bettering the American recycle culture.

Using found trash around RISD campus, I’ve first spray painted the recyclable trash with red and non-recyclable trash with white and lined them up as if they are precious artifacts in a museum. Each red objects are personified and labeled with numbers, and viewers can read their different life stories in a pamphlet.

INSTALLATION

PAMPHELT
The pamphlet starts with the introduction and contains 10 different life stories of trash. At the bottom right corner of each page has an illustration according to the type of trash.

POSTCARDS
Using images and quotes from the pamphlet, I've designed a series of postcard for takeaways.
